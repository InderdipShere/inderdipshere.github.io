It's Me !!!

I am working with the CNRS, Montpellier, a southern city in France. Yes, I am a scientist by profession, and it feels good. I am sure I will be a researcher in my possession in any profession in the future. The future behavior is predicted from the trajectories of my previous works and inclination (driving force) towards exploring things.

For a short duration, I have worked as Research Assistance with my Ph. D. group during the pandemic. This duration helped me publish my final articles and train new members of the group. Definitely like to thank my Ph. D. advisor and IRCC for supporting my position.

The Ph.D.ship was my last aspect of my studentship, and I have been a student almost my entire life. Trained with the intention to explore things and learn to be wrong were a few critical aspects of my Ph. D. The thing that itched my mind is science is a simpler explanation of behavior around us. The better we understand it, the simpler we can explain it. Another interesting thing that challenged my belief was working with random numbers in the Monte Carlo process. It was challenging for me to accept the order behavior of random numbers.

Doctorate in Chemical Engineering

I was awarded Ph.D. from Chemical Engineering Department of IIT Bombay, Mumbai. The theme of my was about developing a molecular simulation tool to capture polymerization systems. We developed an algorithm based on the reaction ensemble Monte Carlo to perform polymerization reactions.

s

We developed various in-house codes to calculate interesting properties like percolation length and porosity of irregular and not periodic structures. We also developed coarse-grained potential for the silica system, which is useful for studying nanoparticle self-assembly with fewer computational resources.

Masters in Chemical Engineering

I received a Master in Chemical Engineering (M Chem Engg) from ICT Matunga, Mumbai, for the work on Pulse Sieve Plate Extraction Column (PSPEC) under the guidance of Prof. V K Rathod. We used experiments and simulations to develop a model representing axial dispersion in PSPEC.

I had an opportunity to do an internship at IPCA, Ratlam, Madhya Pradesh. Wherein I worked with a water treatment facility and prepared the compliance report for the government of Madhya Pradesh.

Bachelors in Chemical Techonology (Surface Coating)

My Bachelor's degree is from Chemical Technology of LIT Nagpur, with a specialization in paints and surface coating technology. As a part of a project, I developed green paints that don't require solvent during their formulation and application. The idea was to use the liquid binder (alkyd based) in the formulation, thereby eliminating the need for environmentally harmful solvents.

I also did an internship in the paint shop of TATA motors Pune, wherein we had first-hand experience with automotive coatings.
