Background & Problem

The Challenge

Research institutes generate significant food waste daily, with facilities like CNRS @ Montpellier producing 10-15 kg of waste every day. At the same time, these institutes have a high and growing demand for ethanol (98° & 70°) for research purposes, currently relying entirely on external sourcing.

Our Solution

ÉthaVriti transforms food waste from research institute canteens into ethanol through fermentation process. This creates a sustainable, circular economy solution that addresses both waste management and solvent supply challenges.

Our Process

Food Waste Collection

Collecting food waste from research institute canteens

Fermentation Process

Converting waste through yeast fermentation process

Ethanol Separation

Distilate ethanol into desired grade

Distribution On-site

Direct distribution within the institute

The scientific behind convertion of food waste into ethanol through enzymatic hydrolysis and fermentation:

\[(C_6H_{10}O_5)_n + nH_2O \xrightarrow{\text{enzyme}} nC_6H_{12}O_6\]

\[C_6H_{12}O_6 \xrightarrow{\text{yeast}} 2C_2H_5OH + 2CO_2\]

Why Choose ÉthaVriti?

  • Waste to Product: Transform food waste into valuable research solvent
  • On-site Production: Zero transportation costs and reduced carbon footprint
  • Dual-grade Flexibility: Production of both 98° and 70° ethanol according to demand
  • Sustainable Solution: Addresses both waste management and supply chain challenges
  • Cost Effective: Significant savings compared to traditional sourcing
