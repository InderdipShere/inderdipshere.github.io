Background & Problem

The Challenge

Research institutes generate significant food waste daily, with facilities like CNRS-ICGM in Montpellier producing 10-15 kg of waste every day. At the same time, these institutes have a high and growing demand for ethanol (98° & 70°) for research purposes, currently relying entirely on external sourcing.

Our Solution

EthaVriti transforms food waste from research institute canteens into high-quality ethanol through an innovative fermentation process. This creates a sustainable, circular economy solution that addresses both waste management and solvent supply challenges.

Our Process

Food Waste Collection

Collecting food waste from research institute canteens

Fermentation Process

Converting waste through enzymatic hydrolysis

Ethanol Production

Yeast fermentation creates high-quality ethanol

Distribution On-site

Direct distribution within the institute

The scientific process converts food waste into ethanol through enzymatic hydrolysis and yeast fermentation:

\[(C_6H_{10}O_5)_n + nH_2O \xrightarrow{\text{enzyme}} nC_6H_{12}O_6\]

\[C_6H_{12}O_6 \xrightarrow{\text{yeast}} 2C_2H_5OH + 2CO_2\]

Why Choose EthaVriti?

  • Waste to Product: Transform food waste into valuable research solvent
  • On-site Production: Zero transportation costs and reduced carbon footprint
  • Dual-grade Flexibility: Production of both 98° and 70° ethanol as needed
  • Sustainable Solution: Addresses both waste management and supply chain challenges
  • Cost Effective: Significant savings compared to traditional sourcing
